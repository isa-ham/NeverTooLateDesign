Ever since high school, I have loved creating responsive and eye catching website layouts. During this time though, I thought I would rather work with children with special needs. So I followed that path, but continued to return to web design over the years.

After several years of trying to balance my interest in technology and working with children, I decided it was time to follow my passion in web design. In 2015, I started to learn coding through Team Treehouse. I gained a solid understanding of HTML and CSS, and started exploring JavaScript. During this time, I was also working at a software testing company. I learned a lot about the agile process and what it is like to develop in a team environment.

In 2016, I went to work for a family member at a clinic as their Education Coordinator. This didn't allow for a lot of time to work on imporving my coding skills, but I did learn a lot about how to work with clients and how to keep stakeholders upto date on projects. One project I started was converting their Google Drive into an EMR.

In 2017, I decided to leave the company so I could follow my passion of web design. After some research into the different aspects of front-end development, I found UI/UX design. UI design really called out to me and I have been persuing it ever since.

I am currently working on my portfolio, and am interested in doing an internship at a web development company. If you have a project you would be interested in having me complete, please do not hesitate to contact me. You can find my contact information below.
